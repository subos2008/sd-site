# Brand name

Working document for `execution/020-brand-name-and-corporate-structure.md`
workstream 1 (the brand name). Status: shortlisting. Nothing here has been
through the knockout screen yet — every candidate is unverified for domain,
trademark, and existing-use until marked otherwise.

## The benchmark: Secret Benefits

Founder's take (2026-07-17): *"Secret Benefits is an amazing name — such a
subtle hint at the sugar side, and 'secret' implies the safety of a secret."*

It is also the incumbent we model our playbook on (Digital Barter Limited,
Cyprus — see `lessons-learned.md`, `founder-anonymity.md`), so the name is
unavailable and anything confusable with it is dangerous twice over:
trademark confusion in the same class, and being read as a knockoff in the
affiliate reviews that are our primary channel. **No "Secret ___", no
"___ Benefits".**

What makes it work — the formula to copy:

1. **Two innocuous everyday words.** Neither word is a category keyword.
   To an outsider it's bland; the meaning only assembles for someone who
   already knows. That deniability is what keeps platforms, app reviewers,
   and bank statements calm.
2. **Word one promises discretion.** "Secret" tells both sides *you are
   safe here* — the supply side's biggest fear is being discovered, the
   demand side's is embarrassment. The safety promise is in the name
   itself, before any copy runs.
3. **Word two hints at mutual gain.** "Benefits" gestures at the
   transactional side (and winks at "friends with benefits") without ever
   saying it. The hint is legible to the target user and invisible to a
   regulator reading for "compensated dating".
4. **It survives a bank statement.** "SECRETBENEFITS" is recognisable to
   the user and merely odd to anyone else — no category word to explain.

## Criteria (from spec 020, restated)

- No category keywords: sugar, arrangement, daddy, baby, spoil, seeking.
  Names that flirt with the keywords inherit the category's platform risk.
- Memorable, spellable after hearing it once, sayable without embarrassment.
  Branded search volume is a headline KPI (brand-harvest SEO), so
  misspellable names leak search traffic.
- Works as a discreet billing descriptor.
- .com available or acquirable; UK/EU trademark screen clean for dating /
  social-networking classes.
- Does not lock us into one positioning; leaves the verification/discretion
  white space open.

## Tests every survivor must pass

| Test | Pass looks like |
|---|---|
| Bank statement | Descriptor reads like a boring company; nothing to explain to a partner or bank clerk |
| Say-aloud | A user can tell a friend the name without flinching |
| Spell-after-hearing | Heard once in conversation → typed correctly into a search bar |
| SERP-ownable | We can rank #1 for the exact name; not a generic phrase with an entrenched SERP |
| Outsider-blind | A platform reviewer or journalist sees nothing in the name itself to object to |
| Insider-legible | The target user gets the wink without a footnote |

## Avoid list

- **"Secret ___" / "___ Benefits"** — incumbent confusion (trademark class
  overlap + knockoff perception in affiliate listings).
- **Category keywords and near-keywords** — sugar, sweet, spoil(ed),
  arrangement, seeking, daddy, baby, allowance.
- **Escort-adjacent vocabulary** — tryst, favours, velvet rooms,
  courtesan, rendezvous-as-noun. These collapse the dating framing the
  legal position rests on.
- **Existing dating/adjacent brands** — Mutual (LDS dating app), Whisper
  (anonymous-secrets app), Keeps (DTC hair brand), Patron/Patreon,
  Hinge/Bumble-alikes. The knockout screen must check every candidate
  against live dating-industry use, not just trademark registers.
- **Hardship framing in any name** — nothing that gestures at money-for-
  need (see the student-demographic guardrails in `README.md`).

## Knockout screen (to run per candidate)

1. .com exact-match: available / parked / in use? Acquisition estimate.
2. UK IPO + EUIPO trademark search, classes 45 (dating/social
   introduction) and 9/42 (app/software).
3. Live dating-industry use: search "<name> dating", app stores, affiliate
   review sites.
4. Unfortunate meanings: slang dictionaries, other languages of likely
   expansion markets.
5. Social handle availability (X primarily — the one open ad platform).
6. Billing-descriptor mock: how does it truncate to 22 characters?

Survivors go to the say-aloud / spell-after-hearing test with real humans,
then decide. Deliverables per spec 020: the name, the .com registered
(registrant withheld, multi-year, Cloudflare-fronted), a trademark filing
decision (default: file UK at minimum), and a **neutral operating-company
name unconnected to the brand** (workstream 2 — the company is *not* named
after the brand).
