# Supply-side (female signup) delivery channels

Researched 2026-07-17 (agent sweep). Closes the supply-side blind spot
flagged in [partner-space.md](partner-space.md): can partners deliver
women signing up, not just paying men? Answer: **yes, but only through
the category's own niche ecosystem — not through mainstream dating CPA
networks.** Source URLs live in the agent findings; the load-bearing
ones are inline below.

## The market price of a female signup

The category's brands run **gender-split in-house programs**: a small
CPL for registrations (including women) and a large CPA reserved for
male purchases. Publicly visible benchmarks:

- **SugarDaddyMeet:** $2 per confirmed registration, $60 per paid
  member, $150 per MALE order, 90-day cookie (affpaying.com listing).
  The existence of a separate male-order rate means the $2 covers
  female signups.
- **Seeking (Reflex Media, partners.reflexmedia.com):** $2 CPL + 45%
  CPS (affpaying — the same previously-unverified listing; still treat
  rates as needing direct confirmation).
- **SugarDaddy.co.uk:** indexed affiliate-page text says affiliates
  "earn with both male and female signups" on CPA and CPL — the
  clearest UK-specific female-signup payout claim found (page blocks
  bots; confirm directly).
- **MySugarDaddy (msd-affiliates.com):** CPA/RevShare/Hybrid on any
  purchase; claims a ~75%-female user base.

**Nobody publicly pays a premium for female leads.** Supply is bought
cheap (~$2/confirmed registration) or acquired by the product itself
being free for women.

## The four routes, with verdicts

1. **Mainstream dating CPA networks — WEAK.** No gender-split rate
   cards or female-reg offers found at CrakRevenue, Cpamatica, Mobidea,
   Advidi, ClickDealer, or TrafficPartner; CrakRevenue's Seeking offer
   guidance literally says "target mature, high-income males (30–55)".
   Do not expect networks to deliver our supply side.
2. **Supply-side content/SEO ecosystem — STRONG.** A tier of niche
   sites ranks for "how to be a sugar baby" / "sugar baby UK" and
   already monetizes female-intent traffic with affiliate links:
   **SugarDating101.com** is the best proof point (10+ years of
   sugar-baby advice content, cloaked affiliate links to Secret
   Benefits, Seeking, SugarDaddyMeet, WhatsYourPrice and others);
   plus whatisasugarbaby.com, thedatingvibes.net, and a cluster of
   UK-keyword microsites (sugardaddyuk.org, uksugardaddy.uk, ...).
   Operators are pseudonymous/anonymous — on-site contact forms only.
   Also: an industrialized **placed-listicle channel** in city/alt-weekly
   media ("best sugar daddy sites for sugar babies" at Washington City
   Paper, Cleveland Scene "Partner Corner", East Bay Express, Vocal
   Media) — agency-placed affiliate articles targeting female search
   intent; the placing agency could not be identified from the pages.
3. **Competitor in-house behavior — STRONG** (the gender-split
   programs above). Confirms female leads are bought and prices them.
4. **Non-affiliate channels reaching UK women 18–24 — WEAK as partner
   channels.** The proven volume mechanism is Seeking's in-house "Sugar
   Baby University" PR/content machine (75k UK student registrations in
   2017; later claims of ~500k UK students; Let's Talk Sugar content
   arm) — corporate PR, never partner-operated, and the highest-risk
   channel in the category (RichMeetBeautiful's university OOH was
   seized/banned in Europe; UK student press covers the topic
   hostilely). TikTok explicitly prohibits sugar-dating content in ads
   AND organic community guidelines — accounts get purged; do not
   build supply acquisition on it. No influencer/UGC agency with
   documented sugar-category work was found, and no UK student media
   network accepts the category commercially.

## Implications for our plan

- **The both-sides launch partner probably does not exist as one
  entity.** Mainstream networks can't deliver supply; supply-side
  content sites don't control demand-side money keywords. Expect to
  assemble both sides from the same *ecosystem* but different
  counterparties: demand-side revshare deals with the review-shelf
  sites ([uk-review-sites.md](uk-review-sites.md)) + supply-side deals
  with the sugar-baby content sites. Update the spec 030 candidate
  profile accordingly — "both sides handled" is the aspiration, a
  two-deal structure is the likely reality.
- **A small, verification-gated supply-side CPL is affordable and is
  the category's proven mechanism.** At the ~$2–4 market rate, 500
  verified activated female profiles ≈ £1–2k total — bounded cash, big
  density payoff, and our verification-by-default gate is exactly the
  anti-fraud control the original channels.md design wanted. This
  reverses the blanket "no CPL" position in
  [affiliate-program-terms.md](affiliate-program-terms.md) for the
  supply side only (founder decision — it is the one cash outlay in
  the model).
- Our "free for women" product economics are themselves the affiliate
  angle for supply-side content sites (it's how they pitch Secret
  Benefits today).
- The Sugar Baby University-style student PR machine is a proven but
  hostile-press channel; anything student-facing stays within the
  guardrails in marketing/README.md ("The student demographic") and is
  in-house, reactive-ready, never partner-run.
