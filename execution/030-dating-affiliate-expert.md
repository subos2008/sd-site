## The deal we are offering

Revised 2026-07-17 (was rev-share-only; the market runs on payout menus
and our real constraint is "payouts funded from realized revenue, cash
exposure capped", not any single instrument):

- **A menu, per `marketing/affiliate-program-terms.md`:** lifetime
  revshare (50%, 65% founding premium) for SEO/content owners; hybrid
  small-CPL + revshare tail (the Seeking shape); or capped CPA per sale
  with a milestone ladder that releases behind realized spend. Not
  offered: $140-class big bounties until beta LTV data exists.
- **All payouts net of refunds and chargebacks** — dating chargeback
  rates make this clause non-optional, and stating it up front signals
  we know the vertical.
- Partner brings **both sides**: supply-side audience reach (women
  signing up — the differentiator; consumer traffic alone is the easy
  half) and consumer traffic (paying men).
- We bring: the product (verification-by-default trust story, PWA,
  UK-first), transparent attribution and reporting the partner can verify
  (spec 100), approved creatives support, and a founding position in a
  new program — early partners get the best terms and the uncontested
  shelf space.
- **No broad exclusivity for a launch-sized commitment.** Time-boxed or
  geography/channel-boxed exclusivity is negotiable; "UK exclusive
  forever" for one launch campaign is not.

## Why a partner, not a hire

The right partner brings, on day one: placements on the review sites that
own the category's money keywords (or ownership of those sites — the best
case), knowledge of real market revshare/CPA rates, fluency in the
vertical's fraud patterns (lead spoofing, incentivised signups, fake
verification attempts), and credibility at the events where deals happen.
Paying for that with revenue share aligns everyone: they earn nothing
unless attributed users pay, and we owe nothing until revenue exists.

Must-haves: UK reach; willingness to work within revenue-funded
structures (someone who insists on big prepaid bounties or upfront
budgets is the wrong partner for this launch); supply-side audience
access; clean methods (see vetting).

## Term-sheet parameters (to negotiate, not to concede up front)

- Revshare %: anchor to what competitor programs pay (space research);
  a new program with no conversion history typically has to beat the
  incumbent rate, not match it. Consider a launch premium (higher % for
  the first N months) instead of a permanently rich rate.
- Duration: lifetime revshare is the vertical's convention and a strong
  draw; a generous time-box (e.g. 24 months per referred user) is the
  compromise position if lifetime feels heavy.
- Attribution: ref-code/cookie window, last-click vs first-click,
  postback support — must match what spec 100 implements; the contract
  and the code must agree.
- Reporting access: partner-visible dashboard or scheduled statements;
  numbers net of refunds/chargebacks; audit right.
- Approved-creatives clause, actively enforced — rogue affiliate copy is
  an ASA liability that attaches to us. Non-negotiable.
- Payout terms: monthly, NET-30, minimum threshold; payouts need the
  same banking rails as the payments workstream.

## Vetting

- Track record specifically in dating (mainstream or adjacent), not just
  e-commerce/SaaS — the vertical's economics and fraud patterns are
  distinct.
- References from operators they've run traffic or programmes for;
  verifiable placements they claim credit for.
- Comfortable with our compliance posture: UK/ASA rules, enforced
  approved creatives, verification-by-default, attribution audits —
  anyone who bristles at anti-fraud measures is self-identifying.
- No history attached to fake-review farms or the spammier end of the
  vertical (search their name/company through the affiliate communities).
- Expect them to diligence US: a revshare partner is investing their
  traffic in our conversion rate. Beta metrics, the verification story,
  and a working checkout are our side of the pitch.

Do not treat any list of names as pre-vetted; the vertical has plenty of
operators whose methods are exactly what the approved-creatives regime
exists to keep out.

## Process

1. Space-mapping research (running): UK review-site owners, network
   programs, competitor affiliate terms, conference dates. Output feeds a
   named candidate list (10+) with vetting notes.
2. Outreach to the top candidates in parallel — the pitch: founding
   partner position in a new UK program, revshare at/above incumbent
   rates, verified-users trust story, transparent attribution.
3. 2–3 serious conversations; where a candidate is more expert than
   partner, a short paid consult ("review our plan, tell us what's
   wrong") is cheap diligence that doubles as recruitment.
4. Tracking pilot during beta (spec 100): partner verifies our numbers
   end-to-end before committing traffic.
5. Sign by Fri 28 Aug (LAUNCH.md partner-ready gate); placements and
   campaigns scheduled for w/c 14 Sep.
6. Fallback if no partner signs: run the review-site listing motion
   ourselves on the same revshare terms (weaker distribution, same deal
   shape), and keep candidates warm for a post-launch signing once beta
   metrics exist.
