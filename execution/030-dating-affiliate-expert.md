# 030: Find a dating-vertical affiliate marketing expert

Status: not started. Research-and-recruitment work, not code. Per
`marketing/channels.md`, an experienced dating-vertical affiliate manager
is probably the highest-leverage marketing hire in the whole plan — the
affiliate channel is a relationship business and the relationships are the
asset. This spec is about finding that person (or firm), and considering
them as a launch partner rather than just a hire.

## Why a partner, not just a hire

The right person brings, on day one: existing relationships with the
review sites and affiliates who own the category's money keywords, knowledge
of real market CPA/CPL rates (so we don't overpay to be taken seriously or
underpay and be ignored), fluency in the vertical's fraud patterns (lead
spoofing, incentivised signups, fake verification attempts — directly
relevant to our gated supply-side CPL design), and credibility at the
events where deals actually happen. That asset is worth more than a salary
suggests, and the structures worth considering reflect it: retainer plus
performance, revenue share on affiliate-originated revenue, or a small
equity/advisor arrangement for a genuine launch partner who opens their
network. Start fractional/consulting; convert to deeper commitment once
they have demonstrated placements.

## Where these people are (starting points)

Venues and institutions, in rough order of usefulness:

- **The vertical's conferences.** TES Affiliate Conferences (the
  long-running dating/adult-adjacent affiliate event, typically Prague and
  Cascais) and Affiliate World (Barcelona/Dubai/Bangkok) are where dating
  affiliate managers, networks, and operators congregate. iDate (the
  dating-industry business conference) is operator-side rather than
  affiliate-side but is where dating-company marketing people are found.
- **CPA networks with dating verticals.** CrakRevenue, Advidi, Mobidea,
  ClickDealer, Cpamatica, TrafficPartner and the dating smartlink
  operators employ account managers and vertical leads who know every
  serious dating affiliate in Europe. Two uses: their staff are themselves
  candidates (poachable, or moonlighting as consultants), and their
  account managers can be asked directly "who runs the best dating
  programmes you work with?"
- **Affiliate communities and job boards.** STM Forum and affLIFT are the
  paid communities where professional affiliates live; both have job/
  service marketplaces and are searchable for dating-vertical threads.
  AffiliateFix is the free tier of the same world.
- **Dating-industry trade press and consultancies.** Global Dating
  Insights (trade news; its coverage names the industry's operators and
  marketers), Online Personals Watch, and the small consultancies that
  serve dating operators (e.g. Courtland Brooks has been advising dating
  businesses for years) — useful both as sources of referrals and as
  candidates for a consulting engagement themselves.
- **LinkedIn, searched precisely.** "Affiliate manager" plus the named
  networks above, or plus incumbent operators (Reflex Media, Ruby Life,
  Together Networks, Dating Group / SocialDiscovery) — people who have run
  dating programmes at scale list it.
- **Reverse-engineering (from channels.md #1).** Pull the referring
  domains of secretbenefits.com and seeking.com; the agencies or
  individuals managing those placements are findable through the affiliate
  disclosure pages and contact details of the review sites themselves.
  The person who built a top review site in this vertical may be the
  single most useful partner available — they own the shelf space.

Do not treat any list of names as pre-vetted; the vertical has plenty of
operators whose methods (fake reviews, spam, non-compliant creatives) are
exactly what our approved-creatives regime exists to keep out.

## Vetting

- Track record specifically in dating (mainstream or adjacent), not just
  e-commerce/SaaS affiliate management — the vertical's economics and
  fraud patterns are distinct.
- References from operators they've run programmes for; verifiable
  placements they claim credit for.
- Comfortable with our compliance posture: UK/ASA rules, enforced
  approved creatives, gated supply-side CPL with clawbacks — anyone who
  bristles at anti-fraud gating is self-identifying.
- No history attached to fake-review farms or the spammier end of the
  vertical (search their name/company through the affiliate communities).
- UK/European market knowledge preferred; the UK review-site pond is the
  one we need first.

## Process

1. Compile a candidate list (10+) from the starting points above; note
   which are individuals, agencies, or network staff.
2. Short consulting conversations with 2-3 (paid, scoped: "review our
   affiliate plan in marketing/channels.md and tell us what's wrong with
   it") — cheap diligence that doubles as recruitment.
3. Pick the engagement structure (retainer/rev-share/advisor equity) based
   on who they are and what they bring; define success as verifiable
   placements and tracked activations, not activity.
4. If a genuine launch partner emerges (someone who brings placements to
   launch day), negotiate that as its own deal — it changes the launch
   plan's Phase 0/2 affiliate groundwork from cold outreach to warm
   introductions.

## Interactions

- `marketing/channels.md` #1 — this person owns executing that channel.
- Spec 020 — the programme needs the brand name and entity to sign deals.
- The payments workstream — affiliate payouts need the same banking rails.

## Acceptance criteria

- A shortlist with vetting notes exists; at least two paid consulting
  conversations completed.
- An engagement (or a documented decision not to engage yet) with defined
  success metrics: placements live, activations attributed, fraud rate
  within bounds.
